Game 1: The Filter Bubble

Goal: Fast-paced physical sorting where the rules suddenly change.

Mechanics & Rules

  • Teams sprint to sort RED and BLUE items from a central bin.
  • The Twist: At 2 minutes, referee yells "Algorithm updated! Only YELLOW!"
  • If a player brings back an old color, they drop it on the grass (penalty).
  • Scoring: +10 pts for correct items, -5 pts for incorrect items.

Swipe/Press DOWN for Details

The Filter Bubble: Details

  • Materials: 1 Large Bin, 200+ Mixed Colored Items, 2 Empty Buckets.
  • Referees: 1 Main Ref (time/rules), 2 Bucket Refs (guard buckets).
  • Reflection Link: "Did you notice how frustrating it was when the rules changed? Algorithms put you in a bubble based on hidden rules... Who is writing the algorithm for your heart? (Proverbs 4:23)"

Game 2: Blind Code

Goal: Navigating a physical maze blindfolded, ignoring the "Viruses."

Mechanics & Rules

  • One player is blindfolded. Their "Programmer" stands on a chair and yells commands.
  • The Twist: Opposing team acts as "Viruses," screaming fake instructions to confuse the runner.
  • If the runner hits an obstacle, they must restart.

Swipe/Press DOWN for Details

Blind Code: Details

  • Materials: 10-15 Blindfolds, 30+ Obstacles (cones/noodles), 2 Chairs.
  • Referees: 1 Main Ref. Team Leaders act as safety spotters.
  • Reflection Link: "How hard was it to hear the right voice? The world's algorithm acts like a loud virus... What viruses do you need to mute to hear God?"

Game 3: Echo Chamber

Goal: Chaotic dodgeball that shows how one idea takes over a network.

Mechanics & Rules

  • Standard dodgeball rules, but no one is eliminated.
  • The Twist: When hit, you instantly switch sides and play for the team that hit you.
  • The game cascades until one side absorbs the other (an Echo Chamber).

Swipe/Press DOWN for Details

Echo Chamber: Details

  • Materials: 10-15 Soft Dodgeballs, Boundary Cones.
  • Referees: 1 Main Ref to enforce team-switching.
  • Reflection Link: "If you only follow people who think exactly like you, your feed becomes a massive echo chamber. Are you open to listening to others?"

Game 4: The Viral Trend

Goal: High-energy water sponge relay to build morale and cool down.

Mechanics & Rules

  • Teams line up. Front player dunks a giant sponge in a water bucket.
  • Pass the sponge OVER the head, then the next person passes UNDER the legs.
  • Last person squeezes the water into an empty bucket and runs to the front.

Swipe/Press DOWN for Details

The Viral Trend: Details

  • Materials: 2 Giant Sponges, 4 Large Buckets, Access to a hose.
  • Referees: 1 Ref to enforce over/under rule. Leaders refill water.
  • Reflection Link: "Like that sponge, we pass on what we consume. Are you passing on a viral trend of kindness, or gossip and negativity?"

Game 5: The Firewall

Goal: Attack & defense game representing protecting the mind from influences.

Mechanics & Rules

  • Team A defends a middle zone (The Firewall).
  • Team B grabs "Data Packets" and sprints to cross the firewall.
  • Defense: If tagged inside the firewall, Team B must drop the packet and run back.

Swipe/Press DOWN for Details

The Firewall: Details

  • Materials: 40 "Data Packets" (tennis balls), 4 Buckets, Cones.
  • Referees: 1 Ref for time and tag calls.
  • Reflection Link: "Your mind is a firewall. The world constantly tries to push 'data packets' past your defenses. Are you an active firewall?"

Game 6: The Pattern Trap

Goal: A frantic Tic-Tac-Toe sprint combined with quick thinking.

Mechanics & Rules

  • Relay style: Players sprint to place 3 items in a 3x3 grid.
  • The Twist: Once all 3 items are placed, the next runners must sprint out and move one of their existing items.
  • Teams constantly reposition items to block opponents and align 3-in-a-row.

Swipe/Press DOWN for Details

The Pattern Trap: Details

  • Materials: 9 Hula Hoops, 6 Colored items (3 per team).
  • Referees: 1 Ref to declare winner and reset the board.
  • Reflection Link: "Social media traps you in patterns. Sometimes we get stuck in bad habits. To win, you had to stop running blindly and break the pattern."

Phase 2: Going Viral

4-Way Chaos Capture the Flag

Setup: Teams combine into 4 Networks (42 players each). Field is divided into 4 quadrants.

  • Players sprint into enemy quadrants to steal Data.
  • Glitch Rule: If tagged in enemy territory, you are "Frozen" and must stand with hands up.
  • A teammate must tag you to unfreeze you.

Swipe/Press DOWN for Details

Going Viral: Details

  • Materials: 80 "Data Items" (4 colors), 4 Data Centers (Boxes), Boundary Cones.
  • Referees: 4 Referees (one per quadrant to enforce "frozen" rules).
  • Reflection Link: "When something goes viral, it influences everyone it touches. What kind of influence are you spreading in your friend groups?"

Grand Finale: System Reboot

The Ultimate Mega-Relay

All 12 teams line up in parallel lanes. Every player participates.

  • Stage 1: Clear Cache. Pop 10 balloons.
  • Stage 2: Reroute Cables. Crawl under nets.
  • Stage 3: Secure Connection. Balance water on a frisbee.
  • Stage 4: Write New Code. Assemble Proverbs 4:23. First to shout it wins!

System Reboot: Details

  • Materials: 120 Balloons, Crawling nets, 12 Cups/Frisbees, 12 Envelopes with puzzles.
  • Referees: 6 to 12 Referees spread across the lanes to ensure no cheating.
  • Outcome: This physically exhausting race ends with them shouting the core verse, leading perfectly into dinner.
